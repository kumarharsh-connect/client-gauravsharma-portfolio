ABOUT ME

Gaurav Sharma

Author, Director-Ghaziabad Literature Festival, Entrepreneur

Hi, I am Gaurav Sharma Lakhi, an author and entrepreneur passionate about storytelling. My journey as a writer has led me to publish eight widely acclaimed books, five in English and three in Hindi. I often explore themes that resonate deeply with human experiences and emotions in my novels. My aim is to connect with readers on a personal level, inviting them to reflect on their own lives and the world around them. My latest work, CRUSH NO. 43, is particularly close to my heart. It tells the story of a poet named Rhythm, who navigates the complexities of life and love through his encounters with various women. This narrative reflects my belief in the transformative power of love and the profound impact that relationships can have on personal growth. One of my earlier novels, LOVE @ AIR FORCE, is often celebrated as one of the best books set against the backdrop of the Indian Air Force. Other significant titles of mine include RAPESCARS...They Never Heal, DAWN AT DUSK, and IT ALL HAPPENED IN A SCHOOL. In addition to novels, I have also written an anthology of children's stories called UNBUDGETED INNOCENCE and poetry books like वहीदा and धर्मेन्द्र, inspired by the legendary actors Waheeda Rehman and Dharmendra. As the director of the Ghaziabad Literature Festival, I am committed to fostering a vibrant literary community and encouraging new voices in literature. I hope this captures the essence you were looking for!

BOOKS

LOVE @ AIRFORCE

Love @ Air Force by Gaurav Sharma follows Wing Commander Shabd Mishra, who is in unrequited love with his school crush Soumya Mahrishi, now married to another officer, and Sergeant Sushil Awasthi, a discontented soldier facing feelings of inadequacy. The novel explores themes of love, friendship, and the rigid military hierarchy as Shabd navigates his emotions and Sushil confronts his frustrations. With a blend of lighthearted moments and poignant reflections, the story offers insight into the personal challenges of Air Force personnel, enhanced by Shabd's poetic expressions and heartfelt letters.

DAWN AT DUSK

Dawn at Dusk by Gaurav Sharma follows Aradhaya, a sheltered millionaire whose life is turned upside down after losing his parents and being betrayed by his wife. In his search for healing, he falls for Sambhavi, a writer and professor, who challenges him to prove himself. Distraught, Aradhaya leaves his privileged life to live among those with fewer means. Inspired by his love for Sambhavi, he embarks on a transformative journey of self-discovery. The story explores themes of love, personal growth, and resilience as Aradhaya seeks Sambhavi's acceptance and a new purpose in life.

RAPESCARS

Rape Scars... They Never Heal follows Akriti, a rape survivor whose parents insist on reporting the crime despite police attempts to undermine the case. She faces humiliation from a doctor and a defense lawyer who questions her integrity, making her feel victimized again. Though her lawyer secures a conviction, Akriti surprises everyone by refusing to imprison her attacker, raising questions about her agency versus trauma. The novel powerfully voices survivors' struggles against societal and legal injustices while emphasizing Akriti's resilience in reclaiming her identity.

IT ALL HAPPENED IN A SCHOOL

It All Happened In A School by Gaurav Sharma is narrated by Harjeet Singh, a disillusioned teacher in a private school. The novel addresses serious issues like child safety and moral decay in education, starting with the tragic sexual assault of a young girl, which raises questions about educators' responsibilities. The story explores the emotional and psychological impacts on students and teachers, revealing the harsh realities of modern education. Sharma critiques the educational landscape and reflects on the loss of innocence, making it a poignant exploration of the darker aspects of school life.

CRUSH NO. 43

Crush No. 43 by Gaurav Sharma follows Rhythm, a celebrated poet who views every woman who impresses him as a 'crush,' believing their emotions inspire his poetry. Orphaned twice and adopted by a notorious goon, he maintains a positive outlook on life. His adoptive parents, Shankar and Usha, provide him opportunities while shielding him from Shankar's criminal past. Rhythm writes women-centric poems but struggles to find true love until he discovers Dr. Vasudha, who is infatuated with him. The novel explores his relationships and the lasting impressions of the women in his life, celebrating the complexities of love and personal growth.
